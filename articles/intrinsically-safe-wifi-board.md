# Intrinsically Safe WiFi Board: Wireless Platform for Hazardous Industrial Equipment


## What Is an Intrinsically Safe WiFi Board?

An Intrinsically Safe WiFi Board is an embedded wireless communication platform designed for industrial equipment used in hazardous environments.

Unlike standard wireless boards, intrinsically safe WiFi boards are designed with specialized power and circuit protection to limit electrical energy and improve safety in areas where explosive gases or dust may exist.

They are commonly integrated into explosion-proof terminals, industrial tablets, handheld devices, and customized wireless equipment.


## Applications of Intrinsically Safe WiFi Boards

Intrinsically safe WiFi boards are widely used in:

- Explosion-proof handheld terminals
- Industrial tablets
- Hazardous area communication equipment
- Oil and gas applications
- Chemical industry equipment
- Industrial IoT devices
- Customized OEM wireless products


## Key Features of Intrinsically Safe WiFi Boards


### Intrinsic Safety Design

Intrinsically safe WiFi boards are designed for hazardous environments with features including:

- Intrinsic safety power protection
- Over-voltage protection
- Over-current protection
- Short-circuit protection
- Reverse polarity protection


### High-Performance WiFi Connectivity

Modern intrinsically safe WiFi boards support advanced wireless technologies such as WiFi 6 and MIMO technology.

These features provide:

- High-speed wireless communication
- Stable network connectivity
- Improved wireless coverage
- Multiple device support


### Flexible Embedded Interfaces

Industrial WiFi boards provide flexible integration options, including:

- Ethernet interfaces
- UART
- RS485
- Mini PCIe expansion
- 4G/5G cellular module integration

These interfaces allow OEM manufacturers to develop customized industrial wireless devices.


## Wireless Networking Functions

Intrinsically safe WiFi boards can support multiple wireless modes, including:

- Access Point (AP)
- Client mode
- Mesh networking

They can be used as embedded wireless platforms for industrial communication systems and IoT applications.
