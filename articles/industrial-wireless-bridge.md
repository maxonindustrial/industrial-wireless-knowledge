# Industrial Wireless Bridge: Long-Distance Wireless Networking for Industrial Applications


## What Is an Industrial Wireless Bridge?

An Industrial Wireless Bridge is a networking device that connects remote Ethernet networks through wireless communication.

It is designed to replace traditional wired connections such as Ethernet cables or fiber links in applications where cable installation is difficult, expensive, or impractical.

Industrial wireless bridges are widely used for long-distance data transmission between buildings, industrial sites, and distributed equipment.


## Applications of Industrial Wireless Bridges

Industrial wireless bridges are commonly used in:

- Factory automation networks
- Industrial Ethernet extension
- Remote equipment monitoring
- IP video surveillance systems
- Oil & gas facilities
- Transportation infrastructure
- Industrial IoT applications


## Key Features of Industrial Wireless Bridges


### Long-Distance Wireless Transmission

Industrial wireless bridges support point-to-point (PTP) and point-to-multipoint (PTMP) networking for reliable communication over long distances.

They are suitable for connecting remote industrial facilities, production areas, and monitoring systems without additional cabling.


### High-Speed WiFi Connectivity

Modern industrial wireless bridges support advanced WiFi technologies, including WiFi 5 and WiFi 6, providing high throughput and stable wireless connections for industrial data transmission.

MIMO technology improves wireless performance, coverage, and connection reliability.


### Industrial-Grade Outdoor Design

Industrial wireless bridges are designed for outdoor and harsh environments with features including:

- Rugged enclosure
- IP68 protection
- Wide operating temperature range
- Strong anti-interference capability
- PoE power supply
